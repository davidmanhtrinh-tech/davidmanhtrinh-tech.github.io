Analysis of Myoungae Kim’s Interactive Experience

This webpage presents my analysis of Myoungae Kim’s interactive web experience.

Initial Interaction

1. What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience?

The first thing that immediately captured my attention was the structured grid of numbered boxes presented on the homepage. The uniform layout created a sense of order, while also suggesting that each box contained something unique to uncover. This contrast between structure and mystery encouraged me to begin interacting with the interface.

2. Spend two minutes with the experience and create a list of each of your discrete actions.

During my two-minute interaction, I moved my cursor across the grid to scan for visual feedback. I clicked on several numbered boxes, observed the changes in content, and then returned to the main grid. I repeated this process multiple times, selectively choosing boxes based on curiosity and pattern- while occasionally pausing to glimpse at and interpret the interaction.

3. What part of the experience did you spend the most time engaging with?

I spent the most time engaging with the process of opening and exploring the numbered boxes. Each interaction revealed different content, which encouraged me to continue navigating through the grid. The repetitive nature of this function combined with the understanding that there were different projects hidden behind these boxes, created a playful and experiential experience.

4. What was the most common action in your two minute interaction?

The most frequent action was clicking on the numbered boxes to reveal hidden content. This action became the core interaction mechanic, as it was consistently repeated and directly tied to progressing through the experience.

Purpose and Interaction Design

5. What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experience?

The primary goal of the experience appears to be presenting the designer’s work and identity through an interactive and exploratory format. Rather than displaying information in a linear way, the experience encourages users to actively uncover content, making the process more engaging and personal. As oppose to our conventional recognition of websites, this site stands out tremendously due to it's unpredictable nature and unsuspecting functional and interactive qualities that force the user to be attentive and engaged.

6. How does the interactive experience communicate this primary goal?

This goal is communicated through the use of concealed content within the numbered boxes. By requiring the user to click and explore, the experience shifts from passive viewing to active participation, reinforcing the idea of discovery as a central theme.

7. What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time?

The experience seems designed to be interacted with in short, repeated bursts. Users are encouraged to return to the grid, open different boxes, and gradually build an understanding of the content over time rather than consuming everything at once.

8. How does the interactive experience communicate how it should be interacted with over time?

The grid-based layout and the large number of individual boxes suggest that the experience is not meant to be completed in a single linear sequence. Instead, it promotes non-linear exploration, allowing users to navigate freely and engage at their own pace.

References and Reflection

9. What other media forms (digital or otherwise) does the experience reference?

The experience strongly references the structure of an advent calendar, where individual sections are opened to reveal hidden content. It also draws similarities to archival systems or digital portfolios, where information is segmented and accessed selectively.

10. What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should act?

These references suggest that I should approach the experience with curiosity and patience. Rather than rushing through it, I am encouraged to explore each element individually and treat each interaction as a small discovery.

11. What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should feel?

The references create a sense of anticipation and playfulness. Each interaction carries a subtle sense of reward, making the experience feel engaging and slightly unpredictable.

12. What is the most frustrating part of the interaction?

The most frustrating aspect of the interaction is the lack of immediate clarity in navigation. Without clear guidance or hierarchy, it can feel slightly disorienting at times, especially when trying to understand the overall structure of the experience.

13. What is the most satisfying part of the interaction?

The most satisfying aspect is the moment of revealing content within each numbered box. This interaction creates a sense of discovery and reward, reinforcing engagement and motivating continued exploration. The experience was reminiscent of Advent chocolate calendars where I would pinch a specific numbered box and uniquely shaped chocolate surprise would appear.
